Welcome to a fan page dedicated to arguably the greatest football team in all of world football. They have become synonymous with prestige and glory.

No other team in world football has reached the pinacle of club football as many times as Real Madrid. In the last 5 years, they have not only eclipsed other teams in terms of the number of Champions League trophies they have won, but they also became the first team in modern football to with 3 championships in a row.

Take a look back at Real Madrid's more recent victories to becoming the Champions of Champions.

For the second time in 2 years, Real Madrid faced off against cross town rivals Athletico Madrid hoping for a repeat result of the 2014 final, where Real came away with their 10th Champions Leauge Victory.

The venue for the 2016 final was the San Siro in Milan, with both teams fielding the exact same 11 players from the 2014 final. The stage was set for history or revenge.

Real Madrid scored first from a free kick in the 15th minute that was put in by club captian Sergio Ramos. Athletico response came in the 79th minute from Yannick Carrasco who was able to score from a corner.

With almost equal possesion across the game, the game went into extra time and then penalties where Real would be victorious by 5 goals to 3.

Real Madrid were back in the final after fending off European heavyweights Bayern Munich and Athletico Madrid. In the final they faced an team seen in the same category as Real in terms of history and grandure. Juventus were looking to take victory from a Spanish team for the second time in 2 years, after losing to Barcelona in the 2015 final.

The Millenium Stadium in Cardiff, home of Real Madrid's Garath Bale was the stage for this epic battle of two European champions.

Despite dominating early possesion, Juvenutus fell behind in the 20th minute from a Ronaldo goal. They were able to respond quickly from an incledible goal from Mario Mandžukić.

The second half was dominated by Real and Juvenutus finally gave way to three unanswered goals. Casemiro, Ronaldo and Marco Asenscio all scored on the way to recording back to back Champions League trophies for Real Madrid.

Not since Bayern Munich in 1974-76 had a team won the European championships 3 years in a row. In the way of Real Madrid was a talented and resurgent Liverpool.

The Olimpiyskiy Stadium in Kiev would host this historic night for Real Madrid and the Champions Leauge.

Liverpool started the game by attacking relentlessly. Mid way through the first half, Mo Salah was subbed off due to a dislocated shoulder, disrupting Liverpool. By the end of the fist half, the game was scoreless, with Madrid dominating possesion.

Early in the second half, Karim Benzema was able to put Madrid infront, with Liverpool answering with a goal of their own from Sadio Mane within 4 minutes. Super sub for the night, Garath Bale came on and scored 2 minutes later from an acrobatic bicycle kick. He went on to add another 83rd minute to put the game out of reach for Liverpool.
